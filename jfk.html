John Fitzgerald Kennedy

JFK, or better known as John Fitzgerald Kennedy, was elected the 35th president of the United States of America. He served from January 20, 1961, until his tragic assassination on November 22, 1963. Known for his inspiring speeches, he advocated for civil rights, space exploration, and global peace during his presidency. JFK was a charismatic president yet a simple man on the inside. His interestes were but were not limited to; tennis, basketball, football, golf and reading. JFK's presidency, marked by the Cuban Missile Crisis, the Peace Corps, and civil rights advocacy, left a lasting legacy. His tragic assassination in 1963 shocked the nation, but his vision and leadership continue to inspire. Information source: Wikipedia, The White House (.gov) and JFK Library
